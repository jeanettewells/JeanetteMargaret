about...



INTERDISPLINARY ARTIST. CREATIVE THINKER. COLLABORATOR.



My name is Jeanette Margaret Wells and I currently attend the School of the Art Institute of Chicago. I aim to explore the dualities of both physical and emotional planes in the broader context of the intersections of art and technology.

I am experienced in visual communication, branding, typography, concept development, drawing, intermediate coding, and collaboration, among many other disciplines. As a dedicated and open-minded team member, I would be a strong addition to any collaborative environment.

My body of conceptual work is the reconstruction of aftermath. It incorporates clean, architectural, design-based elements to investigate the origin of experience through a secondary source. Using design as a tool for detachment, my work speaks primarily about human experience and what results from it.

My work focuses largely on the illness of my deceased father, something that originated physically but has created a series of incorporeal effects. By creating ephemera drawn from my experiences, I recreate a feeling or emotion as a tangible thing, rather than represent the origin of that sensation. I aim to create a sense of removal from my own emotions by using the memory of a computer to parallel how my own memories exist. I create in various media, but most of my work results from the outcome of a combination of several types of practices. However, the majority of my work has originated in a digital realm that has, in some way or another, become physical. In this, I take the elusiveness of emotions and memories and make them material.

about the sunflower...



Since my dad has passed, the sunflower has grown to become a symbol for his lasting impact on my entire family. Not only does it represent his brightness in my life, but it mutually stands for our togetherness in his loss. Losing my father so early has shaped who I am, what I create, and why I create. The sunflower serves as a reminder of both his presence and lack there of.
